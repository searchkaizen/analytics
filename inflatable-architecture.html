Inflatable Category
Architecture Restructure

Strategic objective
This plan gives the inflatable category a proper parent hub, assigns each major buyer-intent cluster to one URL, removes internal competition, and adds new pages only where demand is proven and no page exists yet. The aim is straightforward: get buyers onto the right page and make it easier for them to request a quote.
Current performance constraints

The category is generating impressions it cannot act on

The inflatable category already earns significant organic impressions. The breakdown happens after the click. Traffic is landing on the wrong pages, and many of the pages that do rank give buyers no clear next step toward a quote. The problem is structural.

Strategic moves

Four things this restructure does

1
Establishes a real category hub
The existing hub page is too narrowly framed for broad category queries. Building /collections/custom-inflatables as the parent, with grouped product sections and links into child collections, gives the category a usable entry point for both search engines and buyers.
primary target: "custom inflatables" · 3,000 vol · $1.50 CPC
2
Eliminates internal competition
Two product pages compete with their own collection pages for the same queries. Redirecting them consolidates authority onto the stronger URL in each case. The tunnels collection (pos 7.1, 660 inlinks) and arches collection (pos 9.2, 1,996 inlinks) should both benefit once that overlap is removed.
2 redirects · inlinks consolidated · collection positions expected to improve
3
Houses the replica cluster properly
Right now, 8,759 monthly impressions are landing on a /pages/ URL with no H1 and no product context. Building /collections/inflatable-product-replicas moves that traffic onto a proper collection page that can actually support conversion.
2,150 keyword vol · 8,759 impr absorbed · 0.1% CTR → target improvement
4
Creates pages only where demand is clear
New pages are built for the race arch cluster ($4–$5 CPC, no current URL) and for individual mascot tunnels, where competition is light and order value is high. In several cases, competitors are ranking with gallery items and minimal copy. That is a beatable standard. These are high-ticket custom orders, so a single won deal can justify the page.
race arch $4 CPC · mascot tunnels $3.5K–$5.5K avg order · 8 new URLs total
Expected outcomes

What this is designed to achieve

These are directional. Rankings depend on crawl timing, content quality, and competition. There are no guarantees, but each change is tied to a specific problem in the current structure.

Ranking alignment
Each query cluster gets one clear destination. Right now some pages compete with each other. Removing that overlap gives each URL a better chance to rank for the job it is meant to do.
Improved CTR on existing impressions
The hub page earns 47,231 impressions per month at 0.1% CTR. That means it is appearing, but searchers are not finding the result compelling. A hub that matches what "custom inflatables" searchers are actually looking for should lift that response.
Cleaner RFQ paths
Price-intent queries (tunnel cost, pricing, "for sale") currently land on pages with no pricing content. Adding a pricing callout and clear quote CTAs converts these bottom-funnel signals into quote requests.
New query coverage
Race arch and mascot tunnel pages address clusters currently earning zero clicks because no relevant page exists. The demand is confirmed in keyword data and competitor SERP presence.
Cleaner crawl structure
Fewer duplicate signals, cleaner URL types, and a clear collection hierarchy make it easier for search engines to follow the structure and assign authority correctly.
Durable taxonomy
The two-level collection structure is durable. Additional mascot tunnels or new arch variants can slot into it without another restructure.
Risks & dependencies

What this depends on working correctly

⚙
Custom Shopify collection template
The new hub requires a custom collection template to render grouped product sections with representative SKUs. Without it, the page falls back to a flat product grid and loses the structure this plan depends on.
↗
Redirect chain hygiene
Check Shopify's existing redirect list before adding new ones. A chain (A→B→C) bleeds equity at every hop. Each new redirect should resolve as a direct 301.
⏱
Crawl and indexing lag
Redirect effects on existing pages usually show in GSC within 30 to 60 days. New pages need to be indexed first, so they should be submitted manually and monitored.
📸
Mascot tunnel pages require real product photos
Individual mascot tunnel pages (bulldog, cougar, tiger) need actual MVP Visuals build photography to compete in Shopping SERPs and image results. Low competition is the advantage here, and real build photos make that advantage more useful.
⚑
Sports-inflatables decision deferred
/collections/sports-inflatables is not redirected as part of this restructure. On-page signal cleanup happens now. The later repurpose-versus-redirect decision depends on post-change GSC data.
